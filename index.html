About Me

Bridging Criminal Justice and Data

My academic journey began in the field of Criminal Justice, where I developed a deep appreciation for patterns, behavior, and structured investigation. Over time, I became fascinated by how data — when interpreted correctly — can drive powerful decisions, whether in law, business, or everyday life.

Curious about the intersection of technology and human behavior, I enrolled in an intensive AI Bootcamp, where I learned to apply data analysis, machine learning, and programming to real-world challenges.

Today, I’m exploring career paths in data analytics, AI, and SQL-based insights, building solutions that bring clarity, automation, and smart decision-making to complex problems. I’m especially excited by how tech can be used to improve systems — from justice and governance to education and communication.

When I'm not coding, I'm learning new tools, participating in online AI courses, and applying my growing skillset to passion projects and open-source collaborations.
